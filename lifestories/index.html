A Portfolio of Life Stories

Where is Happiness?

Dear Reader,

This portfolio of five stories written over the course of a semester has been a journey of several purposes. Of course in one part, “Life Stories” has been my first and last LAIS 400 course, but it is also the one of the last classes of my college curriculum — possibly one of the most difficult.

This brings me to the second aspect of the journey; this class has challenged me to rediscover writing. Whereas previously writing only served as a tool for communicating and conveying technical ideas, I have found that writing can also be used for sharing stories. In addition, my perception on stories has changed drastically. Where once before I thought of stories only for their entertainment value, now after reading stories, listening to stories and being mindful of the stories around me, I can see that they are so much more. They define how we act and think about the world around us. They influence us, change us and mold us in ways I never thought possible before.

With that, I would like to share this compilation of stories. Although the stories were initially created as individual entities, each with their own ideas, I have found that many of the stories hold the same core to them: my ideals. Of course it makes sense as I wrote them all, but it had never occurred prior to compiling these stories that I had injected my opinions so firmly at the heart of my writing. With an even further look, there is a hidden, but nevertheless present, theme throughout these stories that almost boils down to: What is it that brings one happiness? It is a truly interesting question, one that we all at one time or another are sure to face ourselves. But what is even more interesting, is that it doesn’t really have a concrete answer. Is it hard-work? Is it self-sacrifice? Is it religion? Is it our outlook on life? Is it perhaps love? Something else? Or perhaps a combination? Or is it just a fruitless search for the nonexistent? Almost inadvertently, I have explored some of these thoughts in the following stories. I’m not out to persuade anyone that I have found the key to happiness, because I most certainly haven’t, nor was/is that even my intended goal, but rather these stories serve as a self-discovery of my own questionings. Maybe you’ll find them thoughtful, maybe you won’t, but hopefully you’ll consider them and ask yourself, “What is it that makes one happy?”

Andrew
